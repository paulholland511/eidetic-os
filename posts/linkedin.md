# LinkedIn

---

I've been building something over the past few weeks that I think points at where personal productivity is heading.

The frustration that started it: AI assistants are brilliant but stateless. Every conversation begins from zero. Last week's research, the reasoning behind a decision, the context you painstakingly built up — gone the moment you close the tab. We've all got this incredibly capable tool with no memory.

So I built **Eidetic OS** — a personal, local-first "operating system" that turns Claude into something that actually remembers and acts on its own.

What it does:

→ Captures every AI conversation back into a private knowledge vault, automatically, twice a day — so research, decisions, and planning are preserved permanently, not lost between sessions.

→ Makes that vault searchable by *meaning* through a local RAG pipeline. Months later you can ask "what did we conclude about X?" and get the real answer.

→ Runs scheduled work autonomously overnight — indexing, briefings, reports — so you wake up to a system that's already done a day's housekeeping.

→ Runs entirely on your own machine. Your notes and data never leave it unless you explicitly choose otherwise. No telemetry. For anyone who takes privacy and data ownership seriously, that's the whole point.

The thread running through all of it is **institutional memory for an individual** — the compounding context that makes a long-tenured colleague valuable, but for your own AI-assisted work. In my world at Skill Capital, where the edge is in what you remember about a market, a thesis, or a conversation six months ago, that compounding context is everything.

It's open source under MIT — on GitHub and PyPI (`pip install eidetic-os`). I'd genuinely love feedback from anyone building in this space, and contributions are welcome.

The future of personal productivity isn't a smarter chatbot. It's a system that remembers everything you've done and keeps working while you sleep.
